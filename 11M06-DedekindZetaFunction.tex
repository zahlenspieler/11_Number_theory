Let $K$ be a number field with ring of integers $\mc{O}_K$.  Then the Dedekind zeta function of $K$ is the analytic continuation of the following series: 
$$\zeta_K(s)=\sum_{I\subset\mc{O}_K} (N^K_{\Q}(I))^{-s}$$
where $I$ ranges over non-zero ideals of $\mc{O}_K$, and $N^K_{\Q}(I)=|\O_K:I|$ is the norm of $I$.

This converges for $\Re(s)>1$, and has a meromorphic continuation to the whole plane, with a simple pole at $s=1$, and no others.

The Dedekind zeta function has an Euler product expansion,  $$\zeta_K(s)=
\prod_{\fr p}\frac{1}{1-(N^K_{\Q}(\fr p))^{-s}}$$ where $\fr p$ ranges over prime ideals of $\mc{O}_K$.   The Dedekind zeta function of $\Q$ is just the Riemann zeta function.
