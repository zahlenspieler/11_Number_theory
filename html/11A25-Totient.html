totient


A totient is a sequence f:{1,2,3,…}→ℂ such that

g∗f=h

for some two completely multiplicative sequences g and h, where ∗ denotes the convolution product (or Dirichlet product; see multiplicative function).

The term ‘totient’ was introduced by Sylvester in the 1880’s, but is seldom used nowadays except in two cases. The Euler totient ϕ satisfies

ι0∗ϕ=ι1

where ιk denotes the function n↦nk (which is completely multiplicative). The more general Jordan totient Jk is defined by

ι0∗Jk=ιk.
