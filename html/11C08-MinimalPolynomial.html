minimal polynomial


Let K/F be a field extension and κ∈K be algebraic over F. The minimal polynomial for κ over F is a monic polynomial m⁢(x)∈F⁢[x] such that m⁢(κ)=0 and, for any other polynomial f⁢(x)∈F⁢[x] with f⁢(κ)=0, m divides f. Note that, for any element κ that is algebraic over F, a minimal polynomial exists (http://planetmath.org/ExistenceOfTheMinimalPolynomial); moreover, because of the monic condition, it exists uniquely.

Given κ∈K, a polynomial m is the minimal polynomial of κ if and only if m⁢(κ)=0 and m is both monic and irreducible (http://planetmath.org/IrreduciblePolynomial).
