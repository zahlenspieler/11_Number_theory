Freiman’s theorem


Let A be a finite set of integers such that the 2-fold sumset 2⁢A is “small”, i.e., |2⁢A|<c⁢|A| for some constant c. There exists an n-dimensional arithmetic progression (http://planetmath.org/MulidimensionalArithmeticProgression) of length c′⁢|A| that contains A, and such that c′ and n are functions of c only.
