proof of Fermat’s little theorem using Lagrange’s theorem


Theorem.

If a,p∈Z with p a prime and p∤a, then ap-1≡1(modp).

Proof.

We will make use of Lagrange’s Theorem: Let G be a finite group and let H be a subgroup of G. Then the order of H divides the order of G.

Let G=(ℤ/p⁢ℤ)× and let H be the multiplicative subgroup of G generated by a (so H={1,a,a2,…}). Notice that the order of H, h=|H| is also the order of a, i.e. the smallest natural number n>1 such that an is the identity in G, i.e. ah≡1modp.

By Lagrange’s theorem h∣|G|=p-1, so p-1=h⋅m for some m. Thus:

ap-1=(ah)m≡1m≡1modp

as claimed. ∎
