transcendental number


A transcendental number is a complex number that is not an algebraic number. That is, it is a complex number that is transcendental over ℚ (or, equivalently, over ℤ).

Well known transcendental numbers include π and e (the base of natural logarithms).

Cantor showed that, in a sense, “almost all” complex numbers are transcendental: there are uncountably many complex numbers, but only countably many algebraic numbers (http://planetmath.org/AlgebraicNumbersAreCountable).
