push-down theorem on class numbers


As in the parent (http://planetmath.org/ClassNumberDivisibilityInExtensions) entry, given a number field K, the class number of K is denoted by hK.

Theorem (Pushing-Down Theorem).

Let E/F be a p-extension of number fields and suppose that only one prime ideal of F is ramified in E and that this prime is totally ramified. Then p|hE implies p|hF.

  • Iwas K. Iwasawa, A note on Class Numbers of Algebraic Number Fields, Abh. Math. Sem. Univ. Hamburg, 20 (1956), 257-258.
