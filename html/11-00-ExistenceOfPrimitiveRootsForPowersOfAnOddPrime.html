existence of primitive roots for powers of an odd prime


The following theorem gives a way of finding a primitive root for pk, for an odd prime p and k≥1, given a primitive root of p. Recall that every prime has a primitive root.

Theorem.

Suppose that p is an odd prime. Then pk also has a primitive root, for all k≥1. Moreover:

  1. 1.

    If g is a primitive root of p and gp-1≠1modp2 then g is a primitive root of p2. Otherwise, if gp-1≡1modp2 then g+p is a primitive root of p2.

  2. 2.

    If k≥2 and h is a primitive root of pk then h is a primitive root of pk+1.
