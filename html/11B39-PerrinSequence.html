Perrin sequence


Construct a recurrence relation with initial terms a0=3, a1=0, a2=2 and an=an-3+an-2 for n>2. The first few terms of the sequence defined by this recurrence relation are: 3, 0, 2, 3, 2, 5, 5, 7, 10, 12, 17, 22, 29, 39, 51, 68, 90, 119, 158, 209, 277, 367 (listed in A001608 of Sloane’s OEIS). This is the Perrin sequence, sometimes called the Ondrej Such sequence. Its generating function is

G⁢(a⁢(n);x)=3-x21-x2-x3.

A number in the Perrin sequence is called a Perrin number.

It has been observed that if n|an, then n is a prime number, at least among the first hundred thousand integers or so. However, the square of 521 passes this test.

The nth Perrin number asymptotically matches the nth power of the plastic constant.
