integer part


The integer part of a real number is the part of the number that appears before the decimal . For example, the integer part of π is 3, and the integer part of -2 is -1.

To be more precise, for x∈ℝ, the integer part of x, denoted as [x], is given by

[x]={⌊x⌋⁢ if ⁢x≥0⌈x⌉⁢ if ⁢x<0,

where ⌊x⌋ and ⌈x⌉ denote the floor and ceiling of x, respectively.
